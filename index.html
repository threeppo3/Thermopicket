    Why we built Thermopicket‚Ñ¢

    Heatstroke is preventable, but hard to see coming.

    By the time you notice dizziness, confusion or nausea, core temperature may already be dangerously high. Most field protocols rely on visual checks and self-reporting, which are slow and subjective.

    ‚ö†Ô∏è The problem

    There is no simple ‚Äúsense‚Äù for heat stress. A person can look fine yet be trending towards heat exhaustion or heatstroke, especially under heavy gear or in high humidity.

    • Heat stress indicators appear in the body before symptoms are obvious.
    • Athletes, cadets and outdoor workers can be away from shade for hours.
    • Supervisors cannot realistically track everyone minute-by-minute.
    • Most helmets, berets and caps only provide sun shade‚Äînot heat monitoring.

    üí° Our approach

    Thermopicket‚Ñ¢ turns the headwear people already use into a continuous, low-friction early-warning system that quietly watches heat trends in the background.

    • Thin HeatSafe‚Ñ¢ band sits hidden in the sweatband, touching the scalp.
    • SentinelClip‚Ñ¢ UV module reads environmental heat load from outside.
    • A single processor fuses body and environment data into a Heat Stress Index.
    • Tiered LED + vibration alerts escalate from ‚Äúcaution‚Äù to ‚Äúdanger‚Äù automatically.

    How it works

    From raw sensor readings to a meaningful heat risk score.

    The firmware inside Thermopicket‚Ñ¢ samples four data streams every few seconds and combines them into a Heat Stress Index that reflects both current conditions and short-term trends.

    üìä Sensor fusion pipeline

    1
    Sample (1‚Äì5 s)
    The band reads scalp temperature, humidity, sweat conductivity and UV signal at a fixed interval.
    2
    Filter & normalise
    Noise and spikes are smoothed; readings are normalised against expected human ranges and local baseline.
    3
    Compute Heat Stress Index
    A weighted formula looks at absolute values (e.g. very high scalp temperature) and trends (e.g. rising humidity + rising sweat conductivity under high UV) to produce a single index.
    4
    Map to alerts
    The index is compared to calibrated thresholds for early caution, heat exhaustion risk and heatstroke danger. The highest level crossed activates LEDs and vibration.

    In a full implementation, these thresholds would be tuned using lab data and real-world testing with athletes and workers in controlled conditions.

    üö¶ Alert ladder & device behaviour

    • üü¢ Normal: LEDs off, vibration off. Conditions are within a comfortable range.
    • üü° Level 1 ‚Äì Early warning: Yellow LED pulse when the Heat Stress Index starts trending upwards.
    • üî¥ Level 2 ‚Äì Heat exhaustion risk: Solid red LED + short vibration bursts suggesting rest, shade and hydration.
    • ‚ö° Level 3 ‚Äì Heatstroke danger: Rapid red flashing + strong vibration, signalling urgent intervention.

    The device always chooses the highest level reached in the last few minutes and will stay alerting until readings clearly fall back into a safer zone.

    If the battery is critically low or a sensor fails, the band can enter a failsafe mode: a brief red flash pattern on startup to warn the user that readings should not be trusted.

    Key features

    Designed for real-world heat, not just lab numbers.

    The focus is on practicality: how it feels to wear, how much effort it adds to a routine, and how clearly it communicates when something is wrong.

    üéß Invisible in daily use

    Sits inside the sweatband of a cap, beret or hard hat. It bends with the fabric and feels like a slightly thicker sweatband rather than a separate gadget.

    More detail
    The band is designed to follow the curve of the head, avoiding pressure spots at the forehead and temples where people are most sensitive to discomfort.
    ü™∂ Lightweight build

    <12 g for the band and <3 g for the clip means almost no change in balance or comfort, even for smaller heads or younger students.

    More detail
    The mass is distributed around the head rather than at a single point, so helmets and caps still feel ‚Äúnormal‚Äù when worn.
    üîã 6‚Äì8 hour runtime

    A single compact Li-Po cell powers both the band and clip for a full training session or typical work shift before needing a charge.

    More detail
    Runtime depends on alert levels ‚Äî calm conditions draw less power than frequent vibration at high risk levels.
    üì∂ One Bluetooth device

    Only the band appears in the app. The UV module is wired in, so users never have to pair or manage a second device.

    More detail
    This avoids confusion for younger users and keeps pairing, naming and firmware updates as simple as possible.
    üß™ Sweat & UV aware

    Thermopicket‚Ñ¢ doesn‚Äôt just read temperature‚Äî it also looks at humidity and sweat conductivity under different UV levels to better approximate true heat stress.

    More detail
    Combining these signals helps differentiate between ‚Äúhot but breezy‚Äù and ‚Äúhot, humid and dangerous‚Äù conditions.
    üõ†Ô∏è Practical materials

    Soft silicone or TPU shell, medical-grade adhesive and stainless-steel contacts are chosen to handle sweat, movement and repeated removal/replacement.

    More detail
    In future versions, different adhesive pads could be tuned for sensitive skin or longer wear sessions.
